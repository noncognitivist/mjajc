God's Creation

To be able to sensibly say anything about discovering God's purpose of giving life, you need to do these few things first:

  1. Define this particular god in a coherent way
  2. Demonstrate this particular god exist
  3. Demonstrate that this particular god gave this life
After these three things are fulfilled, only then it will make sense to talk about this discovering whatever the purpose of life is.

It's not a secret that the Biblical god is diabolical, violent, a war freak, and blood-thirsty. If you are confused or in disbelief, you might want to read the Bible in its entirety (see the page The Naked God for more posts about this god). In the Old Testament in particular, this god actively participated in human affairs and only in a small region in the Middle East. Just like any other gods prevailing in other cultures. The majority of the world, namely the American continent, Europe, Africa, the rest of Asia, and Australia are completely oblivious of this allegedly powerful god's presence. We Filipinos only heard about this god not because of its power and awesomeness, but because of a series of human effort through wars and conquests, violence and atrocities.

God's Genocidal Tendencies and Violent Commands

Religious leaders might want you to believe that the god of the Bible thinks our lives are important, but the biblical narrative says otherwise. A war-freak God Yahweh, casually and repeatedly commanded the Israelites to kill all men in a number of cities: including women, children, old folks, animals like donkeys, just because they serve other gods. See how crazy is that? You might call that a justice because it's god's doing (which is defined as "good"), but what is bad and evil if it isn't? What is evil if killing babies, old people, and unarmed women, pregnant women are not? If God view life as important, why condoned and even commanded these atrocities over petty reasons? Unbelief is a petty reason. No one with sane mind and conscience will order to kill his sons and daughters just because his children think he doesn't exist or they are calling other man "daddy".

If you are biblically inclined and think I am without ground for having no Bible verses to show my position, that is not the case. I can cite Bible verses here that describe how the god of the Bible treated human lives like rags and this page will easily be overran by biblical passages: God's violent tendencies and atrocities against life. He even drowned the whole planet just because men offended him and He knew this would happen, exactly to the minute detail. And still He created man the way He wanted it. Then blame man for it.

Job

In the Bible, we can read an absurd story where God let Satan (whoever that is) kill Job's sons and daughters and livestock to win a bet. Just to win a bet! Imagine that. If this absurd biblical story isn't fictional, what is? If the God of the Bible thinks human life is important, why would he let Job's children be killed just to win a bet? Yes, this god did replaced the dead children, but, really? Like lost toys, you replace children you killed? That is not an act of a good being. Of course people of faith will just pretend this story doesn't exist. But for those who are intellectually honest, this one matters. It's in the Bible.

God's Wrath and Rewards

The God of the Bible only wants obedience. As long as you fear him and obey his whims. Is that a purposeful life? The most important question is, we don't really know which of the three thousand gods is real. Brahman? Marduk? Allah? Thor? Yahweh? Jesus? Juju of the Mountain? Which one? We don't know because there is no verifiable evidence to see and check. If you think the Bible is the evidence, think again. What makes the Bible more real than the Enuma Elish or the Qur'an or The Vedas? There is no reason to believe one of these is evidence. If the Bible is evidence, then the favorite holy books of other religions are evidence too. There is nothing that differentiates the Bible from other holy books. They are all based on stories and narratives. They're ancients. They're all worthy of being in museums. We don't know if there is even a god or gods in the first place so why are we so serious of fighting and arguing which religion serves the right one? Why are we so serious of building our lives around the idea of some god's likes and dislikes? Beings with very improbable, if not impossible, existence.

And even if we consider that somehow the Christian god is real (which is so improbable it is stupid to believe that it is), how do we know which of the 30,000 sects or denominations has the true godhead? The triune, the duality, the oneness, the Arian God, or the Mormon's? Which one? And every last one of these godheads is based solely on biblical narratives, which is very unreliable (read more in The Naked Bible). So again, we can't really verify and examine it objectively since the Bible is vague and not scientific to begin with. Being not scientific in the sense that it is written in an ambigious language, cannot be tested and verified, that is why these division of interpretations exist. Otherwise, there will be a harmonious one doctrine of what and who is god.

The Futile Purpose of Life

And even if we forget about these multiple faces and interpretations of a Christian god, the "purpose" that this religion says is utter senseless. You have to obey what your particular religion wants you to do. You need to live the life that the religion you follow wants you to live. You give them money, in exchange of the promise that you will rise someday after you die to live forever. And what? What will you do with the eternal life that you were promised? To live as a singer to a egotistical god who seems to like to be pampered a lot. Yes, that is your purpose. Your sole purpose is to pamper this god for the rest of your life. To feed your church that promised you life after you die. Then when you die, you'll live forever to sing songs to this invisible, imaginary deity that you were conditioned and indoctrinated to love.

It's a great insult to our intelligence for these religious ministers to expect us to believe these things. The promise of eternal life they told us can't be verified. Unless you're dead. Imagine someone selling you a house that you aren't allowed to take a look and examine. And not only a decent, ordinary house mind you. It is a mansion that doesn't need electricity to opeate. It doesn't need any electricity at all. It's invulnerable, doesn't need maintenance and cleaning. It floats around you and can teleport you wherever you want to be. You can operate every part of it using only your mind. The salesman showed you some copies of documents made by unknown people who died thousands of years ago. These copies are copies of copies of copies made by hand from an original document that doesn't exist anymore. And this copy the salesman showed you is the only "evidence" he has that the house really does exist. And you're only allowed to see it after you die. What's your reaction if someone sells you a house with all those extraordinary qualities? And not just you can visit this awesome house only after you die, the salesman needs you to pay him for the rest of your life. And in addition, you need to be willing to do whatever the salesman wants you to do.

What would you do to such an offer? Of course you'd write a check or draw your money and give it to the salesman. You'll change your lifestyle too just to be able to serve the salesman's bidding and continually give him money until you die happily thinking you are doing all this for the awesome house you were promised. Yes, that is what you're going to do and I know it because that's what you did when your church sold you an empty promise of heaven.

If you really value your life, you need to examine the claims the salesman makes. You need to examine the claims your church makes. In this blog we examine these claims in detail: What is God? Is there any viable, convincing evidence that God exist? Does the Bible makes a convincing evidence of the claim that god exists?
