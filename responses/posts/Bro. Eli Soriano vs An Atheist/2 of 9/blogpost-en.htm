Bro. Eli Soriano #1 Argument: Debunked

Here Bro. Eli Soriano argued that there is something written in the Bible that proves God exists. I wrote a post detailing this particular argument and the refutation titled "Proof of God: Daniel's "Prophecy".

In summary, Bro. Eli Soriano claimed that Daniel 12:4 proves his particular deity exists because it prophesied the invention of televisions, satellites, and cell phones. He overly inflated the verse to mean something extraordinary and pretends it foreknew the advent of modern technology when in fact, the verse only said trivial thing. Bro. Eli Soriano said,

Now, there are things written that prove that there is God. The Bible states some things beyond human imagination during that time.

This is the said verse:

But thou oh, Daniel, shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased.
Daniel 12:4
That's it. Nothing more. The mental gymnastics Bro. Eli Soriano performed just to make this biblical passage sound phenomenal is astounding. How he interpreted the phrase "and knowledge shall be increased to mean the emergence of mobile phones and television is beyond me. Bro. Eli Soriano confidently and proudly proclaimed the biblical god's "foreknowledge" as his proof that there is someone who can see the future, able to predict the spawn of computers, satellites and space stations. Now, let's go back to reality, even my now eight-year-old sister can see that this is not an impressive foreknowledge or something remarkable, nor even worth noting. It's just common sense. Knowledge increases. That's nature. It's like me saying that people are getting older. There's nothing new about it. Nothing worth noting. Maybe if I wrote it in future tense, would someone like Bro. Eli jump at it and call it detailed prophecy? Anyone can watch back the past and intuatively infer that knowledge has been increasing. It is just a simple observation. What Bro. Eli Soriano calls "divine foreknowledge" is actually called common sense. There is no "computers", "satellites", "space stations" in the Book of Daniel or anywhere in the Bible, in any form or description. Why Bro. Eli Soriano is so impressed with "knowledge shall be increased" is beyond me. To claim that it is a "prophecy" and it's "detailed" is akin to lying. And people believed it! That's the amazing part. Not the "prophecy" itself.

Another thing that actually made me laugh a bit is how he went on and describe the evolution of television through the years. With that experience alone, he can actually infer that the knowledge of man increases without the need for some omni-everything god.
