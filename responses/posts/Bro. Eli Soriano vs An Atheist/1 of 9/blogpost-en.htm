His argument is that the computer, even though it doesn't know or incapable of analyzing how it came to be, or who its creator is, it doesn’t mean nobody created it. Likewise, we humans (or our brain) too, have a creator. The two things he tried to make an analogy here, are too dissimilar, so why would that mean they have the same cause. There are a couple of reasons why this analogy is poor and stupid.

Human, which the brain is a part of, are capable of reproducing and is subject to mutation (changes). The computer isn't. If the two are too dissimilar, how they work and what they are composed of, why would we conclude that they are both have a similar origin? Just because they share similarities doesn't mean we can conclude they also share other properties. Otherwise, using the same logic, we can also conclude that the mind is made of electronic parts (because the computer is), that the brain runs Windows or Linux (because the computer does). This alone debunked his argument.
