Bro. Eli Soriano's Argument of "Computers are Complex Therefore God" Debunked

I already answered this argument in the Youtube response video, but I feel like making a post just for this "proof" for readability and accessibility.

Eli Soriano's "brain-computer-therefore-god" argument is so horribly flawed, any rational person will find it very stupid. I will mention the flaws again in this post:

  1. First, the analogy is wrong. The brains and computers have completely different origins, work differently, made up of different materials, etc. So hastily concluding that they should both have creators because they have similar traits is fallacious at best. We know that brains developed from a fertilized egg through biochemical processes which are well-studied by scientists, because they are totally physical and therefore can be tested, examined, and observed. We also know how computers are made too. The processes involved in the development of brain and computers are totally different. We don't know exactly how brain works. We have neuroscience for brain while computer science and engineering for computers, so we can see that the two works very differently, so to say that brains have a creator because the computer has one is wrong. If this argument is valid, then we can also make an equally derived logic that our brains are made in factory, run in electricity, runs an operating system, connect to the Internet, and so on and on. But these are absurd conclusions. If the analogy is wrong, then the whole argument fails.
  2. Second, even if we grant, for argument's sake that our brains are created, how did Eli Soriano knows that it is a god? How does it connect logically that it is his personal deity? It could be just another advanced civilization, or it could also be that we and our brain are just a simulation just like in The Matrix designed by we-don't-know-who-or-what, who knows? The possibilities are endless. Eli Soriano has no proof that it is his god who created our brain, us and everything by extension, other than "because the Bible said so". Eli Soriano and anyone who thinks the Bible can be used as evidence must understand that it is a fallacy to do so. It is a claim. You can't use claims as evidence. The Bible claims there are gods. Logic dictates that claims can't be used as evidence. Again, his argument fails.
  3. Third, even if again, we grant him for argument's sake that it is some deity that created us, how did Eli Soriano knows that it is his specific, personal, omniscient, omnipotent, omni-everything transcendent, unobservable, unverifiable god Yahweh? Why not Marduk? Why not Asshur or Izanagi? Why not Allah? Or Brahman? Why Yahweh? Why not Bathala? Again, Eli Soriano has no proof or answer other than "because the Bible said so". That is not an evidence. Again, his argument fails, as expected.

A God who sucks at creating things can't be called God. Not omniscient. Not omnipotent. Not really perfect as Bro. Eli Soriano wants it to be.

If our complex brain is so complex it must be created by some more complex something (Eli Soriano calls it "God"), then it also follows, by Eli Soriano's logic, that God, being so complex, that it also demands a creator. Now this God's creator is also so so complex, that it too, demands a creator more more complex than it.
