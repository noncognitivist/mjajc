Mr. Controversy X's Free Will

Mr. Controversy X defines free will as "something done without compulsion and with willingness." This is a very vague and even incoherent definition of something "important". Mr. Controversy X may have forgotten to clarify what he meant by "compulsion" in his definition because as his blog post progressed rationalizing how free will means to obey and do what God wants, he apparently missed explaining what qualifies as 'compulsion'. This is so because the verses he cited were plain examples of compulsions in various types of divine extortions.

He contradicted himself when he cited biblical passages to illustrate how his god gave men "free" choice to either worship Him or get destroyed. God-given choice of "obey or die", based on his definition, is never a free choice, or free will. It is a classic example of extortion. Extortion is defined as "the practice of obtaining something, especially money, through force or threats". In this case, God wants obedience and He extorts people into it by threatening them with destruction or death or whatever you think a psychopath can come up with to force his victim to submit to his will. Let us take a look at the cited verses that Mr. Controversy X thinks support his position.

If you are willing and obedient, you will eat the good things of the land; but if you resist and rebel, you will be devoured by the sword.” For the mouth of the Lord has spoken.
Isaiah 1:19-20

Is that really a free choice, a free will, using Mr. Controversy X's definition? It sounds more like some tyrant obsessed with power. But more importantly, the verse tells us that God is not giving men free will, or free choice, but extorting them to obey Him. This is no different to extort someone to give you his money or you'll stab him to death. In this verse, God is compelling men to obey him or they will be devoured by the sword. That is just the exact opposite of free will, which is defined as "no compulsion". Any two-grader will understand this simple distinction.

Let's examine another verse that Mr. Controversy X has provided as support to his claim.

...if your heart turns away and you are not obedient, and if you are drawn away to bow down to other gods and worship them, I declare to you this day that you will certainly be destroyed. You will not live long in the land you are crossing the Jordan to enter and possess. This day I call the heavens and the earth as witnesses against you that I have set before you life and death, blessings and curses. Now choose life, so that you and your children may live and that you may love the Lord your God, listen to his voice, and hold fast to him.
Deuteronomy 30:17-20

Evidently, this verse doesn't really fit the label "free will" now does it? God is compelling the Israelites (and by extension the believers as to what Mr. Controversy X may imply by quoting this) to choose to be obedient to Him and worship Him and to Him alone. This is an example of compulsion. It compels the believers to be submissive to his own biddings or else they'll certainly be destroyed, and their lives cut short. Sounds really like a compulsion to me. Now logically, if it is something done with compulsion, then it is not free will, by definition.

This entire façade of Mr. Controversy X's free will is so flawed and obviously illogical and inconsistent. His God demands obedience. His God will punish anyone who doesn't obey His wants. He will destroy and make them suffer for an everlasting torment just for choosing to disobey. That is hardly a free choice. It is clearly an extortion and Mr. Controversy X wants to dress it as "free will". But no matter how hard Mr. Controversy X put an effort of performing mental gymnastics into making this compulsive God looks loving and compassionate, logical and critical thinkers will see through the mistake.

Mr. Controversy X's "Free Will" Makes Holduppers Pious And Compassionate

Mr. Controversy X's claim about his God, who allegedly gave mankind free will, is demonstrably wrong. How can it be free will if you'll be punished for it if you do otherwise? Consider these very simple scenarios:

  1. A guy comes to a bar and sits near to a lady and tells her he loves her. He says that as a loving and compassionate guy, he will give her the free choice to either love him back and obey or not. It is up to her. But if she chooses not to love him back and obey, he will torture her to death. A very, very, slow and agonizing death.
    2. Does that make any sense to you? Because that is exactly what Mr. Controversy X's logic of free will sounds like.
  2. A robber jumps from behind and points his gun at you. As you about-turn slowly, he tells you that he is giving you the free will, a free choice. Choice between either you give him all your money and all your belongings, or he'll squeeze the trigger and bury a bullet or two inside your skull.
    3. Using Mr. Controversy X's logic, the robber is being loving and compassionate. He didn't just shoot you and killed you on sight. He has given you free choice. It's you who chose not to obey him. You exercised your free will wrongfully. You chose to die. You are guilty of killing yourself. Does that makes any sense to you? Either way, that is exactly how Mr. Controversy X's logic of free will sounds like.
